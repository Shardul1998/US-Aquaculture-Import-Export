## ðŸ“ˆ Key Insights
- **Salmon dominates U.S. aquaculture exports**, followed by **Pacific Salmon and Scallops**, indicating a concentrated export portfolio.
- **U.S. aquaculture imports have more than tripled since 1990**, revealing heavy dependence on foreign producers.
- The **gap between imports and exports continues to widen**, signaling strong domestic growth potential.
- **Tilapia and Atlantic Salmon are heavily imported**, representing opportunities for U.S.-based production.
- **Canada is the leading U.S. aquaculture trade partner**, with strong trade flows from **Asia-Pacific markets** including South Korea, Taiwan, Singapore, China, and Australia.
- **U.S. seafood consumption is steadily increasing**, with forecasts indicating record-high demand.
- A **Market Opportunity Index** shows accelerated growth post-2010, with strong momentum toward **2025**.
- **Atlantic Salmon continues to surge**, while **Coho Salmon faces trade challenges**, suggesting the need to reassess production priorities.
